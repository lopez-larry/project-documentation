---

## 2. Purpose & Background

**Purpose**

* Concise statement of why the project exists
* What decision or problem this project addresses

**Background**

* Business problem or opportunity
* Current state and high-level pain points
* Alignment with organizational strategy and goals

---

## 3. Objectives

* Clear, measurable outcomes the project must achieve
* Prefer SMART objectives where possible
* Focus on *outcomes*, not implementation

---

## 4. Scope

**In-Scope**

* Major capabilities, features, or deliverables included
* Explicit boundaries of responsibility

**Out-of-Scope**

* What is explicitly excluded
* Prevents scope creep and misaligned expectations

---

## 5. High-Level Requirements

* Key system or product requirements at a summary level
* No detailed functional decomposition
* Detailed requirements are defined in the SRS

---

## 6. Milestones & Deliverables

* Major milestones with target dates or phases
* High-level deliverables only

  * Example: Prototype, MVP, Production Release
* Detailed schedules are maintained elsewhere if needed

---

## 7. Assumptions & Constraints

**Assumptions**

* Conditions assumed to be true for planning purposes
* Examples:

  * Resource availability
  * Tooling or platform readiness
  * Stakeholder participation

**Constraints**

* Known limitations
* Examples:

  * Budget ceilings
  * Regulatory requirements
  * Fixed deadlines

---

## 8. High-Level Risks & Dependencies

**Risks**

* Initial, strategic-level risks only
* Examples:

  * Funding risk
  * Resource availability
  * Schedule feasibility
* Detailed risks are tracked in the **Risk Management Plan & Risk Register**

**Dependencies**

* External systems, vendors, teams, or approvals
* Dependencies that could materially impact delivery

---

## 9. Stakeholders

* Key stakeholder roles:

  * Sponsor
  * Project Manager
  * Product Owner (if applicable)
  * Development
  * QA
  * Operations
  * End Users
* Optional:

  * High-level RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed)
* Detailed communication expectations are defined in the **Stakeholder Communication Plan**

---

## 10. Approval & Authorization

* Approval signifies authorization to proceed
* Confirms alignment on scope, objectives, and risks

**Approvals**

* Project Sponsor:

  * Name
  * Signature
  * Date
* Project Manager:

  * Name
  * Signature
  * Date

---

### Charter Guardrails (Implicit but Important)

* This document **authorizes the project**
* It does **not** define detailed requirements, design, or implementation
* Changes after approval must follow the **Change Management process**
